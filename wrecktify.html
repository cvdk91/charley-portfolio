Wrecktify

THE CHALLENGE

Wrecktify was created for my UX Certification. It is a passion project inspired by life in a country that has more bicycles then people. Unfortunately, taking your bike to a repair shop became more difficult during corona lockdowns. My solution? Bringing cyclists and repair experts together online.

My goals were to:

1. Observe the market

2. Understand the needs of potential users

3. Propose a solution

4. Refine the product

5. Add some (visual) pazazz

GOAL 1: OBSERVING THE MARKET

Through newspaper articles, statistics and qualitative interviews I discovered the following:

  • There are more cyclists then ever
  • They are becoming more environmentally conscious
  • They want to stay active
  • They are willing to work hard (cycling through wind and rain) to reach their (moral and physical) goals

    • To better understand the current market, I performed a competitive and S.W.O.T. analyses. They provided the following insights:

  • There are several websites offering free step-by-step bike repair tutorials
  • These target hobbyists or sports-enthused cyclists
  • Content is aimed at people with advanced to expert knowledge

    • The research showed there was an underserved demographic: the commuter.

    GOAL 2: UNDERSTANDING THE NEEDS

    Next up were user surveys and interviews. I wanted to better understand the frustrating situations potential users might encounter. The most important discoveries were:

  • Cyclists would be willing to repair bikes themselves, depending on the effort needed
  • Bike owners enjoy that cycling saves them money, keeps them fit, and allows them to go wherever they want
  • When it comes to repairs, they mainly feel held back by lack of knowledge, tools, motivation and time
  • They are especially wary of being ripped off by bike repair shops

    • Based on this information, I developed 3 user personas to represent the target demographic.

    GOAL 3: PROPOSING A SOLUTION

    After gaining a better understanding of the target demographic, it was time to apply this knowledge. I initially started with a Sitemap to create an overview of potential pages. A card sorting study and a similarity matrix helped me sort these pages into 4 distinct categories.

    Then it was time to start sketching. Starting with the most important features: finding a tutorial, finding a mentor and a mentor profile. For speed and flexibility I started off with low-fidelity wireframes.

    GOAL 4: REFINEMENT

    When several screens were developed, I performed usability tests. A rainbow spreadsheet served as a guideline for further developemnt. After some tweaking I continued with the high-fidelity wireframes in Figma.

    GOAL 5: ADDING SOME (VISUAL) PAZAZZ

    Now that I had a much clearer image of what was expected from the content and flow of the app, it was time to add some personality. Since the app's focus is a Dutch staple (bicyles), I decided to rework an art style that was very distinct for the region: de Stijl. The distinct elements that I used (such as shapes and colours) were reworked for a digital environment. A design language system was used to ensure consistency across platforms.
